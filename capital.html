About Sacramento

Sacramento, the capital city of California, is known for its rich history, cultural diversity, and political significance. It is a vibrant city that offers a blend of historical landmarks and modern attractions.

Population

Sacramento has a population of 526,437.

Year Incorporated

Sacramento was incorported on February 27, 1850, making it one of the oldest incorporated cities in California.

Region

Sacramento is situated in the northern part of California's Central Valley, serving as a central hub for the state's agricultural industry.

Classifications

Sacramento is classified as an urban city, characterized by its bustling downtown area, numerous cultural institutions and vibrant neighborhoods.

Average Income Level

The median household income in Sacramento is about $65,046, which is somewhat lower than the California state median income.

Top Attractions

  1. California State Capitol Museum
    • The mueseum offers exhibits on California's history and governement.
  2. Old Sacramento State Historic Park
    • This area preserves the historic district of Sacramento, with buildings dating back to mid-19th century.
  3. Sacramento Zoo
    • The Sacramento Zoo is home to over 500 animals and offers educational programs and events for visitors of all ages.
  4. American River Parkway
    • The American River Parkway offers 32 miles of scenic parkland along the American River, with opportunities for biking, hiking, fishing, and picnicking.
  5. Tower Bridge
    • The Tower Bridge is an iconic Sacramento landmark, a vertical lift birdge that spans the Sacramento River and connects West Sacramento to downtown Sacramento
